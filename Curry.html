Chicken Curry


Ingredients needed:


  • 3 Tbsp refined oil
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 3 Green cardamom
  • 3 Cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 kg chicken (cut into bite size pieces)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • to taste salt
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 Green chillies (slit)
  • Handful of curry leaves
  • 2 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp cashew nuts - poppy seed paste (Soak 15 cashews and 1 Tbsp poppy seed in a cup of milk for an hour. Grind it into a paste.)
  • 2 tsp chicken masala
  • 2 tsp kasoori methi
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp dhaniya powderfor garnishing coriander leaves, chopped


Directions to cook:


  1. In a wok add oil, to which you add a bay leaf, green cardamom, cloves, cinnamon. Saute until it releases aroma.
  2. Now add onions and saute till golden brown. After this add the ginger-garlic paste. Mix and add the chicken pieces. Saute till they start to change color to brown.
  3. To this add turmeric and salt. Toss and add water.
  4. Add the green chilli and curry leaves. Let the curry simmer.
  5. Add the red chilli powder, cashew nut -poppy seed paste, chicken masala, kasoori methi, garam masala and dhaniya powder. Mix well and let the curry simmer till the chicken is cooked. In this step you can add more water if required.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve with roti or rice.

Paneer Masala Fry


Ingredients needed:


  • 7-8 Paneer Cubes
  • 1/2 cup Curd
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chilli Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 5-6 Curry Leaves1 Dry Red Chilli


Directions to cook:


  1. Small cubes of paneer should be cut.
  2. Now combine the ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder, pepper, garam masala, turmeric powder, and coriander powder in a bowl.
  3. Adding thick curd now, mix the masalas together. Add few lemon drops last.
  4. Add the paneer cubes, making sure to thoroughly coat each piece. Give it 15 minutes to relax.
  5. Heat some oil in a skillet, add the curry leaves, dry red chilli, and allow it to sputter.
  6. Add the paneer chunks and fry them on both sides.
  7. Remove it and serve it when it becomes crispy and brown.
