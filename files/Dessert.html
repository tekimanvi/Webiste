Indian Desserts


The sweet foods that Indians eat are referred to as mithai, and each one has a distinctive flavour character. Commonly used in Indian desserts are basic components like sugar, wheat, almonds, milk, and lentils. However, each one has a distinct flavour and is accentuated with traditional Indian spices. These candies have more purposes than just satisfying a sweet tooth. Some of them are offered to deities and are customary at festive times. Indian desserts are typically prepared on the stovetop, making them a tasty alternative to baked sweets.

Dried Fig Kheer


Ingredients needed:


  • 1 Cup overnight soaked dried figs
  • Handful of rice (soaked for 3-4 hours)
  • 1 litre milk
  • 4-5 tbsp condensed milk
  • 6-7 saffron strands
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder


Directions to cook:


  1. Make a paste out of the soaked anjeer and set it aside.
  2. Make a paste out of the rice and set it aside.
  3. 3. Boil milk for 3 to 4 minutes in a pot over medium heat.
  4. 4. Add the rice paste and stir constantly to prevent lumps.
  5. 5. Add the anjeer paste and stir continuously.
  6. 6. Add condensed milk and saffron, then cook the mixture until it has a creamy, thick consistency.
  7. 7.Add cardamom powder last, combine, and then spoon the mixture into small dishes. Serve.

Besan (Chickpea Flour) Laddoo


Ingredients needed:


  • 2 Cupsgram flour (coarse)
  • 6-8 Cashew nuts
  • 6-8 Almonds
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1/2 tsp green cardamom powder
  • 1/3 cup low-calorie sweetener


Directions to cook:


  1. 1. Combine the almonds and cashew nuts in a food processor and process until coarse.
  2. 2. In a non-stick kadai, melt the ghee. Add the gramme flour and stir until the gramme flour is aromatic and light brown. Normally, this takes 15 to 20 minutes.
  3. 3.Stir to combine the cashews, almonds, and cardamom powder, then turn the heat off in the kadai. Set apart for cooling.
  4. 4. Add the calorie-free sweetener and thoroughly combine with your hands.
  5. 5.When totally chilled, form into walnut-sized balls and place in an airtight container.
