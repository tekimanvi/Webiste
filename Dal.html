What is Dal?


Derived from the Sanskrit word that means “to split”, dal is a collective term for pulses—lentils, peas, and beans. While sometimes used in other dishes, these pulses are usually served slow-simmered into a soft, porridge-like dish that's also called dal. It's one of the most widespread and traditional daily foods across India, Pakistan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh, and like any dish that millions of people cook daily, dal is infinitely adaptable.

Dal Fry


Ingredients needed:


  • Onions - 2
  • Toor Dal(Red Gram Dal ) 3/4 cups
  • Water 3½ cups
  • Tomatoes - 3
  • Green Chillies - 4
  • Curry Leaves - Handful
  • Asafoetida Powder - one pinch
  • Cilantro leaves - handful
  • Ginger - 1
  • Mustard Seeds
  • Cumin Seeds
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Red Chilli Powder
  • Garam Masala
  • Oil
  • Lemon Juice
  • Salt


Directions to cook:


  1. Firstly, simmer 3 cups of water and 1/4 cup of toor dal in a pressure cooker until 5 whistles.
  2. Now, heat one tablespoon of ghee in a separate kadai and sputter one tablespoon each of mustard, jeera, dried red chilli, curry leaves, and asafoetida.
  3. Add one onion, one tablespoon of ginger garlic paste, and one green chilli. Good sautéing
  4. Add an additional 1/4 teaspoon each of turmeric, chilli powder, and coriander powder. On a low flame, sauté.
  5. Moreover, add 1 tomato and cook it until it is mushy and tender.
  6. Add the cooked dal and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix well.
  7. 5 minutes of simmering is plenty to make the dal smooth and flavorful.
  8. Now include 1/4 teaspoon garam masala, 1 tablespoon coriander, and 1 teaspoon crushed kasuri methi.
  9. Redy to serve with rice or roti.

Sambar


Ingredients needed:


  • ½ cup toor dal (rinsed)
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • 3 tsp oil
  • 1½ cup water
  • ½ onion (petals)
  • 1 tomato (cubed)
  • 1 carrot (chopped)
  • 5 beans (chopped)
  • 1 potato (cubed)
  • few drumstick
  • 2 brinjal (chopped)
  • few curry leaves
  • 1 chilli
  • 2½ cup water
  • ½ cup tamarind extract
  • 2 tbsp sambar powder
  • 1 tsp jaggery
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp mustard
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 2 dried red chilli (broken)
  • pinch hing
  • few curry leaves


Directions to cook:


  1. First, combine 1/2 cup toor dal, 1/4 teaspoon turmeric, 1 tablespoon oil, and 1 1/2 cups water in a pressure cooker.
  2. Cook the dal well in the pressure cooker for 5 whistles.
  3. Once the pressure has been released, mash the dal smooth with a whisk.
  4. Now add 2 brinjal, 5 beans, 1 potato, 1 tomato, 1 carrot, 1 onion, and 1 half of an onion.
  5. Add a few curry leaves, a single chilli, and 2 1/2 cups of water.
  6. Stir thoroughly and bring to a boil.
  7. Add 1/2 cup tamarind essence, 2 tbsp sambar powder, 1 tsp jaggery, 1/2 tsp turmeric, and 1 tsp salt once the water has come to a boil.
  8. Make sure to thoroughly blend everything by mixing.
  9. Cover the pot, and pressure cook for 1 whistle
  10. Open the cooker after the pressure has been released, then check the seasoning and if the vegetables are cooked properly.
  11. Heat 2 tsp of oil to prepare the tempering. Add 1 teaspoon each of mustard, urad dal, 2 dried red chillies, a pinch of hing, and a few curry leaves.
  12. Make sure the tempering sputters.
  13. Mix well after adding the tempering to the sambar.
  14. Finally, eat hot, steamed rice with mixed veg sambar.
